> This moment takes place at a park.
> Freshly painted slides, brand new swings, and resplendent trees accompany a sun that fills the skies with flourishes of red, as it departs from the day.
>
> We are here to merely observe and listen, to see what makes us who we are and is our destination truly malleable like water or set in stone as many claim destiny to be.
>
> There are three small boys playing in the park. We shall call them Red, Black, and White.
> For the moment, that is. There is no one else in the park, except for me, by design. 
> 
> This is an experiment?

---

“What we have here is a mission, gentlemen\!”, Black shouted, lifting a fist in honor of the mission.

The other two boys didn’t move a muscle, engrossed in their respective activities.

“I said,” Black took a deep breath, “WE HAVE A MISSION\!”

Red moved his pocket mirror around watching as reflected light danced around his toes. His eyes remained tied to the small circle of light as he repeatedly pushed his hand in front of the mirror and away.

White, on the other hand, traced his fingers along the ground, delicately tracing lines and shapes on the soft soil. 

Black sighed.   
“Guys, come on, we must band together against this brand new threat that destiny has brought us\!”

Red glanced up, puzzled, “What threat?”

Black pointed at the bench.   
“Her.”

Red stared into the distance and spotted Black’s “threat”.   
Even White looked up in curiosity. 

There was a woman seated, dressed in a sharp red coat, her eyes protected by tinted glasses, inspecting a newspaper with abject focus. Next to her lay a large stack of newspapers. She seemed to pay no attention to the boys, the park, or anything for that matter.

“Well, what about her?”, White finally spoke up.

“Oh, what about her? She is a beautiful dame and we must steal those papers.”

“Why..”, Red observed the lady, “and how?”

“Well, she seems rich. Rich people aren’t nice people. Such people must be punished\! Also, we need those newspapers so we can make paper planes out of them. And as for how, with a prank\!”

White meekly spoke up, “I don’t think being rich is an excuse to punish someone.”

“Pfft, rich people lord it over others like they own the street. If you are too chicken for this, you better not be involved in the prank. Red, you in or out?’”

Red pondered, “I am in, and so is White.”

White meekly smiled, grateful to be part of the prank.

Black snorted, “Very well, chicken is included too. I wasn’t gonna leave him anyway. He’s an important part of the whole thing. So here’s how it’s gonna go…”

---

White slowly began his walk up to the lady on the bench. He wished he could walk even slower but it was only a matter of time before he reached her. He didn’t want to reach her.

Okay, he was in front of her.

The lady lowered her newspaper and stared at him. To get a better glance, she lowered her glasses and White noticed her light purple eyes.

“What can I do for you, young man?” She had a sharp voice that pierced the air, making him feel like he had woken up and snapped to attention.  

“Umm, frau, there’s an apple out of reach, umm, over,” White stammered over his carefully memorized lines, that Black had asked him to deliver flawlessly, “ over there. I can’t reach them so, can you help me? To reach the apple? I can take it home and give it to my little sister.” 

He tried to stare at her soulfully but had he ever taken the effort to look in a mirror properly, he would have known, he did have a naturally soulful disposition. 

In the meantime, White widened his eyes and stared at the lady.

Her eyes bore a hole within him, her gaze never changing.   
White felt suffocated, took a deep breath, and shifted his feet.  
But the lady didn’t move a muscle.

After an eternity, she spoke up.   
“Very well, boy. Take me to this tree.”  
She stood up and laid the newspaper aside.  
He was probably half her height.

“Well?”   
White hadn’t expected the plan to work.  
“Ah yes, right this way.”

They stood in front of an apple tree, its branches spreading far and wide, providing shade to any that stood under it. White and the lady stared at multiple apples laying on the ground, ripe and untouched. Then they looked at each other.

“Boy, are you sure you want one of the hanging apples itself?”   
White gulped. “Yes, please.”

Sighing, the lady kicked her heels off, stood on her tiptoes, and reached for one of the apples.

Out of the corner of his eye, White could spot Red and Black slowly creeping out of the bushes, nearing the bench. Oh, but they wouldn’t make it in time\!

“Uhm, not that apple, the other one to your left.”  
The lady stared down at him again.   
White widened his eyes.  
She sighed again and reached for another apple.

White could see Red and Black in the distance, holding heaps of newspapers in their hands.

“Here you go, your precious apple.”   
The lady put her heels back on.  
“Thank you, frau.”  
“So what do your little friends desire to do with my newspapers?”

White froze. “Excuse me, frau?”  
“Your friends, Red and Black, what do they want to do with my newspapers?”

White began to notice what was happening around him.   
The leaves falling had frozen in mid-air. There was silence all around.  
In the distance, Red and Black were paralyzed, the papers slowly falling out of their hands.  
He looked back at the lady. She smiled.  
“We haven’t properly met, have we? My name is Alicia.”

---

Black was extremely confused.  
To his ten-year-old mind, this had been a foolproof plan.   
The lady and White came walking back, the latter’s jaw still hanging open in awe.  
Alicia snapped her fingers and he and Red fell to the ground, covered in newspapers now.

Black spit at the ground, then screamed at the lady, “You’re a witch\!”

Alicia chuckled, “Oh, I wish\! But sorry to disappoint you, no, I am not.”

Red observed her carefully, “What are you then?”

“I am an art student, to be precise.”, Alicia beamed. “And you are all in my art project.”

The three boys glanced at the frozen falling leaves all around in confusion and then back at the lady. Black was at a loss for words and stared aghast at Alicia. Red was caught by the light striking the leave and scattered sunbeams with his hands, passing his hand through them.

White tugged at her hand in joy, however. “I want to be an art student too\! I love drawing. I was just drawing this park over there in the ground a few minutes ago\! Please, you have to see it, I want to know if I can be an art student too.”

Alicia patted his head, “Your interest in art is what made me bring you here, White. You are quite unlike the other two here, in terms of all that you will do in the years to co-” Black interrupted her, “What do you mean, you brought him here? White, do you know this witch?\!”

“Again, young man, not a witch. No, he has no clue of who I am. Yes, I brought him and not just him, I brought you two here as well.”

Black snorted, “Well, this may surprise you but I came here on my own. Didn’t we, Red?”

But Red was silent, pondering what Alicia said.   
“Red?”  
“Well, Black, when I think about it, I don’t really remember how I got here. In fact, the last thing I remember is going to sleep last night. And then I was here, staring at the sunlight dancing off the water in the pond. Do you remember how you came here?”

Black was silent now. A tiny nod from him provided Red with the answer.

Alicia smiled again (she smiled a lot, generally). “Red, you are right of course. I whisked you all off a second after you fell asleep an-” White interrupted excitedly now, “And we are in a dream?” She laughed again, “No, that would be a much simpler explanation but in the days to come, this will remain as nothing more than a faint memory to all of you, a light dream.”

“But as I was saying, I whisked all three of you off a second after you fell asleep and I intend to return you as soon as you do something for me.”

The three boys still stared at her, then erupted into a volley of questions. “But how did you freeze the leaves?\!” “How did you make the park?” “How did you bring us here?” “What do you want me to do?\!” “Where did you get that dress from?” “Why are you pretty?\!” 

Alicia snapped her fingers.   
The boys became silent again, involuntarily, of course.

“I am going to do this once, and this is all you will get out of me. Am I clear?”   
The boys nodded.

“Firstly, in words that you may/may not understand, I come from a different planet in a parallel dimension. We are advanced vastly beyond your species, effectively immortal, capable of telekinesis, perceiving and manipulating time non-linearly, and creation of non-sentient matter. I am an art student at Gloriafete Multi-dimensional University of the Arts. This year, in my studies, we are covering the planet, G-956, or as you call it, Earth, and we are required to submit our Philosophy Projects by Monday. I have no clue why on earth, Philosophy is so darn important to learn about Modern Art, but hey, whatever, makes me pass. I was stuck in a rut trying to figure out what I could do and that’s when this tiny idea of this park and you all came into mind.”

Alicia paused to catch her breath. Red seemed to be the only one who seemed to have a grasp of what she had said, and that was good enough.

“Secondly, for the project itself, I brought you here a few minutes ago and I was going through certain newspapers detailing your achievements in the time you spend on G-956. I will present each one of you, one at a time, with these newspapers, in private, with your actions and the consequences of those actions and I will be recording your reactions. I mean, all of this is recorded as well. Once that is done, I will send you all back to your bed, leaving you with no recollection of anything that has happened over the last few minutes.”

“We shall start with you, Black.”

---

White and Red sat by the pond, still reeling from the whirlwind of a day they had had.   
White sat and attempted to trace Alicia’s face in the soil, whilst Red walked about moving frozen sunbeams out of the way and trying to taste them.

Black, as Alicia had commanded, was first and with her.  

---

**Black opened his eyes. He was lying in his bed. They felt mildly wet like he had been weeping. There had been a strange dream but he couldn’t remember it.**

**He got up out of bed and ran to his mom’s bed.**

**“Charlie, dear, what’s the matte-Oh\!”** 

**His mom was overwhelmed by her son’s strong hug, refusing to let go. Charlie then pulled himself back, stared her in the eye, and firmly said, “Mom, I really want you to know that, I love you, so very much.”** 

**She smiled and hugged him back firmly, “Charlie, I love you, even more.” He was so engulfed in her embrace he failed to notice the glistening film of tears on her eyes.**

**Mother and son stayed in the hug, both refusing to let go that night.**

---

> ### Subject 1
>
> Art Project Name: Black   
> G-956 Name: Charlie Chaplin 
>  
> I showed mischievous little Charlie, the boy who had tried to prank me, the multitudes he made laugh in the decades to come in his existence, whilst he never uttered a word in his brilliant performances. I showed him his famous speech when he rallied the troops against Hitler, a speech that would echo through the minds of children when they rally against the great three dictators in World War IV in 2138\.  
> 
> And yet all he asked me about was his mother. He desired to know if she died at peace, if she was cured in the end. He cried when he learned his mother would die in 30 years. He wanted the world for her.  
> 
> He did not care to know anything more about the future but beseeched me to send him home, so that he could savor his time with his mother. 
>   
> And so I did.

---

White turned around and found Red nowhere in sight. It must be Red’s turn now.

This meant that White was last.

He was nervous, what if he had done something terrible. What if Alicia showed him having killed someone? White began to fidget and draw more furiously. 

Maybe he didn’t want to know the future.

Anyway, it was Red’s turn now.

---

**Red woke upright in bed.**   
**He felt a certain joy within.**

**Time. Light. The mysteries of the cosmos beckoned to him.** 

**He got out of bed and stared outside the window. It was nearly dawn.**  
**The sunrise. Light moved so fast and something about it thrilled him.**

**“Albert\! What are you doing out of bed?”**

---

> ### Subject 2
>
> Art Project Name: Red   
> G-956 Name: Albert Einstein
>
> Albert, the boy who sided with Charlie and stayed observant, was a fascinating human specimen to converse with. He displayed mild concern regarding his personal life in the future, the disheveled lives of his wives and his children, and seemed moderately shocked his older self would behave in such a manner.
> His interest was piqued when I revealed his contributions towards human history, proving Newton’s statement about time incorrect, equations of general and specific relativity, and his subsequent journey towards the unified field equation.
> 
> Despite my insistence to not reveal more about where I come from, he argued that since I would wipe the encounter from his memory regardless, I should be able to answer furthermore of his questions. Worn down by his energy, he stayed enthralled as I described the more boring parts of my existence, how I moved by curving space-time, how matter can be distorted, the structure of black holes, and much more.
> 
> When I got completely tired, regardless of his repeated shouts of protest, I sent him home.

---

**White woke up, bawling in bed. His tears flowed endlessly as he kept crying incoherently, making no sense. He couldn’t even remember why he felt so terribly sad.**

**His mother rushed to his side, trying to calm him down before his father woke up. But White just wouldn’t stop.** 

**His father stormed into the room and hit him, yelling at him for ruining his sleep.**

**White did not cry anymore after that.**

---

> ### Subject 3
>
> **Art Project Name:**  White  
> **G-956 Name:** Adolf Hitler
>
> This was by far, the most fascinating individual in the art project. Maybe I related with him a bit for his fascination with art. It’s a pity of course that he failed art and ended up joining the war. If you are like me, one wonders how one would have turned out had things been different in one’s own case. I definitely do not think I would have been a genocidal dictator.
> 
> I wondered if little Adolf here would have been attached and sympathetic to his family like our Charlie, in pursuit of higher aims like Albert or something else entirely?
> 
> He was relieved when I showed him his father would die in 3 years’ time. But as I waited, I wanted to see the reaction to the bloodshed that was his legacy.
> 
> In the 345 years, I have spent scouring galaxies and meeting people, I have never met anyone who cried so deeply as this boy did. He fell to his knees as I showed him all the terrible things that had occurred as a consequence of his actions. 
> 
> Was it inhumane to show him all of that?   
> I am not sure.
> 
> Did his tears justify the oceans of tears that flowed from the victims of his action, the atrocities that he committed?   
> No, it did not.
> 
> But that boy cried his heart out. It wouldn’t ever make up for the impact that the Holocaust had left upon G-956 but my heart wrenched for a second, nearly making me forget this was an art project. Adolf clung to my clothes and begged me to tell him I was lying, to tell him he could change all of this, to let him keep this memory so he could stop all of this from happening. 
> 
> He never stopped crying even when I finally sent him home.
> 
> I never thought this might break me.

---

> This concludes my art project and written below is the aim of the project.
>
> I aimed to wonder if children would be proud of their older selves and stand by them.
>
> Children are innocent, untouched creatures, devoid of knowledge and driven by simple morals. But can the same be said about our older selves?
>
> Would we be presentable to our younger selves? Would ur younger selves be driven by the same pursuits the older selves are?
>
> Material wealth? Power? Fame? Family? Mysteries?
>
> After my art project, I am honestly still unsure.   
> But then what is the purpose of art but to make us doubt ourselves and our sanity?
>
> I do not think I will return to G-956 by choice in the near future. Humans are mere fascinations and I would like to keep it at that. 
>
> I conclude my submission.
