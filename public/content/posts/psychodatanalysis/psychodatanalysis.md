You know what’s interesting, I always considered myself a bit of an average cinephile. But I also have a countering drive to watch popular movies released recently, just so that I can know what everyone is talking about. Because I believe it makes me a better conversationalist?

However, I maintained I was firmly on the side of cinephile rather than normie as I scoffed internally at my perceived elitism. (Not that there’s anything wrong with watching the lastest blockbuster.) However, looking at the graph, it’s pretty apparent, atleast in 2025, that my attention was skewed more towards recency.

I think it’s not surprising that only one film passed the 9/10 threshold then, and even that film is from 1991.

Random tangent, I think there’s a case to watching the old greats as opposed to the current batch, especially because the old greats have proved themselves in one way, **they survived the decades.**

It takes a special kind of effort to craft a film, a novel, a painting, a work of art that is able to capture an imperceptible truth about society, that one can consume it decades or even centuries later, and say, “That’s me. I am not alone and I have existed in many forms before this, and I will continue to in the century ahead.”

Those works deserve their due attention, and I hope to continue to shower them accordingly.
